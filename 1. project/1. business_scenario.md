# MajsterPlus — Business Scenario

## The Company

**MajsterPlus** is a mid-sized Polish DIY (Do-It-Yourself) retail chain
specializing in home improvement products. Founded in 2015, the company
operates **12 stores** across Poland's major cities: Warszawa, Kraków, Łódź,
Wrocław, Poznań, Gdańsk, Szczecin, Bydgoszcz, Lublin, Białystok, Katowice, and
Gdynia.

MajsterPlus serves a broad customer base — from professional contractors and
serious DIY enthusiasts to occasional weekend renovators. The product range
spans 8 categories: Tools, Paint, Plumbing, Electrical, Garden, Flooring,
Building Materials, and Lighting.

## The Problem

Customer acquisition costs in the Polish retail market have been rising
steadily. MajsterPlus's VP of Marketing, Katarzyna Nowak, has noticed a
concerning trend: **too many customers make one or two purchases and never
return**. The marketing team's current approach — sending identical
promotional newsletters to all customers — is expensive and increasingly
ineffective.

A preliminary analysis suggests that approximately **18% of MajsterPlus
customers are "lapsed"** — they have not made a purchase in the last 90 days.
This represents a significant revenue risk.

## Your Role

You have been hired as a **data analyst** to help MajsterPlus solve this
problem. Your mandate:

1. **Understand the customer base** — Who are MajsterPlus's customers? What
   patterns distinguish active customers from lapsed ones?

2. **Predict customer lapse** — Build a machine learning model that identifies
   which customers are at risk of lapsing, so the marketing team can intervene
   proactively.

3. **Recommend a reactivation strategy** — Translate your model's predictions
   into a concrete business recommendation: Which customers should receive a
   targeted reactivation campaign? What is the expected financial impact?

## The Data

MajsterPlus has provided you with two datasets:

- **`customers.csv`** (~5,000 records) — Demographics, behavior, and engagement.
  Includes a binary target `is_lapsed` (1 = no purchase in 90 days, 0 = active).

- **`transactions.csv`** (~25,000 records) — Transaction records with dates,
  amounts, product categories, and payment methods.

The data was extracted from MajsterPlus's systems on **January 15, 2025**. It
covers customer registrations from January 2022 through December 2024.

> **Important**: Like all real-world data, these datasets contain quality
> issues — missing values, inconsistent formats, and columns requiring type
> conversion. You must identify and handle these issues.

## Business Context for the Reactivation Campaign

MajsterPlus is considering a **targeted reactivation campaign** for customers
predicted to be at risk of lapsing. The proposed approach:

- **Intervention**: Send a personalized 50 PLN voucher via email/SMS and a
  follow-up phone call.
- **Campaign cost**: ~80 PLN per customer (voucher + operations).
- **Expected outcome**: Success leads to the customer returning to their
  historical purchasing pattern for the next 12 months.

The key question: **Is it more profitable to target only high-risk customers, or
to contact everyone?** Your model should maximize the return on investment.

## The Five Mini-Projects

Your analysis follows the **CRISP-DM** methodology:

| MP  | Title                | Phase       | Focus                          |
| --- | -------------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------ |
| MP1 | Context & Explore    | Underst.    | Load, explore, train baseline  |
| MP2 | Clean & Feature Eng  | Prep.       | Handle missing, outliers, enc. |
| MP3 | Baseline Modeling    | Modeling    | Train LR and RF, evaluate ROC  |
| MP4 | Evaluation & Impact  | Eval.       | Cost matrix, profit, threshold |
| MP5 | Final Recommendation | Eval.       | Compare models, write report   |

Each mini-project builds on the previous one. Golden baseline checkpoints are
provided if you fall behind.
