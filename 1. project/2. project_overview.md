# PUM: Designing Services Using AI Methods — ML Mini-Projects

## The MajsterPlus Challenge

You are a **data analyst** hired by **MajsterPlus**, a mid-sized Polish DIY retail
chain operating 12 stores across Poland. Approximately **18% of customers are
"lapsed"** — they haven't purchased in over 90 days. Your job: build a machine
learning model to predict which customers are at risk, and recommend a targeted
reactivation campaign.

Over five mini-projects, you'll follow the **CRISP-DM** methodology:

```text
MP1 → MP2 → MP3 → MP4 → MP5
 │      │      │      │      │
 ▼      ▼      ▼      ▼      ▼
Data   Data   Model  Business Final
Under- Prep   Build  Evaluate Recommend
stand
```

## The Five Mini-Projects

| MP  | Title                 | Phase         | What You Do                    |
| --- | --------------------- | ------------- | ------------------------------ |
| MP1 | Context & Explore     | Understanding | Load, explore, train baseline  |
| MP2 | Clean & Feature Eng   | Preparation   | Parse, clean, handle missing   |
| MP3 | Modeling & Comparison | Modeling      | Train LR/RF, evaluate ROC      |
| MP4 | Evaluation & Impact   | Evaluation    | Cost matrix, profit, threshold |
| MP5 | Final Recommendation  | Evaluation    | Compare models, write report   |

## Your Starting Point

Your baseline model achieves **~0.83 ROC-AUC**. By MP5, you'll have cleaned the
data, engineered features, and compared multiple models to find the one that
maximizes business profit.

## Assessment Model

- **5 mini-projects × 10 MCQ questions = 50 questions total**
- Each test is delivered via Edux with a **48-hour window**
- Questions 1-5: conceptual (theory, methodology, interpretation)
- Questions 6-10: project-specific (interpretation of your results)
- **Before starting each test**, ensure you have computed all key values

## Reproducibility Requirements

All students use the **same dataset** and **same random seed**:

- **Seed**: 42 (set at the top of every notebook)
- **Libraries**: scikit-learn 1.4-1.5, pandas 2.x, numpy <2.0
- **Preprocessing**: Follow the exact 12-step order in MP2

This ensures everyone should get the **same numerical results**.
